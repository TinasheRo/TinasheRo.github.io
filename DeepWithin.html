01 - Problem Space

Students are logging emotions, but never understanding them

  • Students experience significant pressure from coursework, deadlines, financial concerns and balancing personal responsibilities
  • While many well being apps allow users to record emotions, they often fail to help understand emotional patterns
  • As a result, users regularly log emotions without gaining insights into their emotional well being or how they can improve it

"What would have helped me when i felt overwhelmed?"

05 - Design process

A human centred process from day one

The project followed a design thinking process. research informed personas, personas shaped wireframes, wireframes reveaked usability issues, issues drove iteration

02 - Competitor Analysis

What already existed And What was missing.

Headspace, Widgetable, Calm was analysed to understand the competitive landscape. The opportunity was clear: nobody combined mood tracking, sleep data, jorunaling and contextual AI support in a single flow

"Headscape is great but I have to pay to access anything useful it feels like a paywall app, not a wellbeing app" - App store review

06 - User Testing

7 university students


Real words. Real reactions

After completeing the first protoype. I recruited 7 university students to test it using a think aloud protocol - users will navigate the app freely while speaking their thoughts out loud. I sat with each participant an noted exactly what they said as they moved through the screens. No leading questions. No guidance. Just their honest, unflitered reactions.

Results of the User Testing

Discoverability & navigation

What participants struggled on

Users were unsure where to log moods, access history, or return to the journal from the home screen

Evidence

5/7 participants hesitated or clicked around before finding the right entry point

Content Discovery

What participants struggled on

Users wanted session duration visible before opening meditations and found content hard to scan

Evidence

Participants wanted to know length and type of session at a glance

Data visualisation

What participants struggled on

Users wanted trends and graphs rather than isolated entries, so they could understand emotional patterns over time

Evidence

Several participants said they expected a graph or clearer progress view

Feedback & System Status

What participants struggled on

Users wanted confirmation that their mood had been saved and what would happen next after submission

Evidence

Multiple participants expected a success state or clearer transition after logging

02 - Reseach Discovery

Three key themes emerges through user testing of the old prototype. Each theme become a design requirement.

01

Improve Navigation & Feature Discoverability

Users struggled to locate key features such as mood logging, mood history, and the journal, highlighting the need for clearer navigation and more discoverable entry points.

02

Enhance Emotional Progress Visualisation

Users wanted to view mood trends and emotional patterns over time through graphs and summaries rather than isolated mood entries.

03

Strengthen System Status & User Confidence

Users expected clearer confirmation after completing actions and more contextual information (e.g., meditation duration) to feel confident using the app.

07 - Design Iteration

How the app evolved through every decision

Every iteration was a reponse to something learned - from user research, from testing, from asking "does this actually solve the design requirement?" Here is the story of those changes:

01

Navigation and feature discovery iteration

  • The old version
    The old version did not have any visual hierarchy and the background was distracting for users & lack of information hierarchy
  • The new version
    The mood journal becomes it own full page. Stress level, sleep quality, and AI insight are immediately visible.
02

Emotional progress visual iteration

  • The old version
    The orginial prototype shows that the visual hierachy was weak and overwhelmed the users.
  • The new version
    The new prototype shows clear Information architecture, easy access for users to look at old entries and log in check ins.
03

Strengthens system status feedback & user confidence

  • The old version
    The orginial protoype confused users as the system status failed to ensure their check is logged in the system.
  • The new version
    The loading progress ensures users data of their check in is being processed and get confirmation in the end.
